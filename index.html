About

"We are surrounded by data, but starved for insights"

My contact with the “world data” goes back to 2018, as a weighbridge operator in a waste transfer station/recycling centre, where my main responsibility was to collect waste transactions data. Soon enough, I realized the great amounts of daily data generated, its quality and the processes in place to collect it. My first data insight given was to my manager and was about the low data quality collected (and further impact in high-level reports) and solutions to fix this issue. My business case was successful and I was appointed to deliver a new data collection process.

With this, I found a passion for data analysis, and power of insights to improve business efficiencies. During my career, I have been developing my skillset in data analysis in various ways:

  • Proactively supervise and ensure a high level data quality generated in a waste transfer station;
  • Working closely with a data team to ensure high standards in data quality on senior level reports.
  • Participation in numerous data projects such as:
    • Centralized digitalization of documentation; production of dashboards for fast and frequent insights;
    • Deliver of new data collection software; data quality audits in end reports and pointing out improvements to improve data quality;
    • Set up of internal dashboards (in Microsoft Excel and using PowerQuery) to assist in data-driven decisions and to provide data for senior level reports.

After being introduced to programming languages by a friend, I’m now entirely dedicating myself to the data analysis career path by learning Python and SQL and developing my capacity to produce insights with Tableau.

It has been an challenging but amazing journey that keeps feeding my eternal hunger for learning 😊.
